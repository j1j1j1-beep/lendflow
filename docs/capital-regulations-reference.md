# Capital Module — Regulatory Reference (2026)

All statutes, thresholds, and requirements verified from sec.gov, law.cornell.edu, ecfr.gov, and irs.gov as of February 2026.

---

## 1. Securities Exemptions

### Regulation D — Rule 506(b) (17 CFR 230.506(b))
- **No general solicitation or advertising** permitted
- Unlimited accredited investors
- Up to **35 non-accredited investors** (must be "sophisticated" — able to evaluate merits/risks)
- Non-accredited investors must receive disclosure documents comparable to Reg A
- Issuer must have **reasonable belief** investor is accredited (self-certification acceptable)
- Bad actor disqualification rules apply (Rule 506(d))
- No SEC qualification/registration required
- No dollar limit on offering size

### Regulation D — Rule 506(c) (17 CFR 230.506(c))
- **General solicitation permitted** (advertising allowed)
- **ALL purchasers must be accredited investors** — zero non-accredited
- Issuer must take **reasonable steps to verify** accreditation (self-certification NOT sufficient)
- Verification methods (non-exclusive list per Rule 506(c)(2)(ii)):
  - Income: review IRS forms (W-2, 1040, K-1) for prior 2 years + written rep for current year
  - Net worth: bank/brokerage statements + credit report dated within 3 months
  - Professional certification: Series 7, Series 65, Series 82, or CFA
  - Written confirmation from registered broker-dealer, SEC-registered investment adviser, licensed attorney, or CPA
  - Existing investor re-verification: if invested as accredited in last 5 years, written rep of continued status
- Bad actor disqualification rules apply

### Regulation A (17 CFR 230.251–263)
- **Tier 1**: Up to **$20 million** in 12-month period. State blue sky review required. No ongoing reporting.
- **Tier 2**: Up to **$75 million** in 12-month period. State blue sky preempted. Ongoing reporting (annual, semiannual, current event reports). Non-accredited investor limit: 10% of greater of annual income or net worth.
- Requires SEC qualification (Form 1-A filing)
- Audited financials required for Tier 2

### Regulation CF — Crowdfunding (17 CFR 227)
- Maximum **$5 million** in 12-month period
- Must use SEC-registered funding portal or broker-dealer
- Non-accredited investor limit: **$124,000** aggregate across all Reg CF offerings in 12 months (2025 inflation-adjusted threshold)
- Annual report (Form C-AR) required
- Resale restrictions: 12-month holding period

### Rule 504 (17 CFR 230.504)
- Up to **$10 million** in 12-month period
- No specific disclosure requirements (but anti-fraud rules apply)
- Generally restricted securities unless registered under state law

## 2. Accredited Investor Definition (17 CFR 230.501(a))

### Individuals
| Criterion | Threshold | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Income | **$200,000** individual / **$300,000** joint with spouse or spousal equivalent | Each of prior 2 years + reasonable expectation for current year |
| Net worth | **$1,000,000** | Excluding primary residence. Include: stocks, bonds, real estate (non-primary), cash, business equity |
| Professional certifications | Series 7, Series 65, Series 82, or CFA | Added in 2020 amendments |
| Knowledgeable employee | Directors, executive officers, or advisory committee members of the fund | Rule 3c-5(a)(4) |
| Family office | With at least **$5 million** in AUM | Must direct the investment |

### Entities
| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Bank, insurance company, registered investment company, BDC, SBIC | Automatic |
| Employee benefit plan (ERISA) | **$5 million** in assets, or investment decisions by bank/adviser/registered dealer |
| Corporation, partnership, LLC, trust | **$5 million** in assets, not formed for specific purpose of acquiring securities |
| Entity where all equity owners are accredited | Automatic |
| SEC/state-registered investment adviser | Automatic |
| Rural Business Investment Company | Automatic |

**No inflation adjustment has been made to the $200K/$300K/$1M thresholds as of 2026.** The SEC's 2023 Dodd-Frank review recommended considering adjustments but no rule has been adopted.

## 3. Qualified Purchaser (15 U.S.C. § 80a-2(a)(51))

Required for Section 3(c)(7) funds:
| Type | Threshold |
|------|-----------|
| Natural person | **$5 million** in investments |
| Family company | **$5 million** in investments |
| Trust (not formed for specific purpose) | **$5 million** in investments |
| Institutional investor | **$25 million** in investments managed on discretionary basis |

"Investments" defined in 17 CFR 270.2a51-1: securities, real estate held for investment, commodity futures, financial contracts, cash and cash equivalents held for investment.

## 4. Investment Company Act Exemptions

### Section 3(c)(1) (15 U.S.C. § 80a-3(c)(1))
- Maximum **100 beneficial owners**
- No requirement that investors be accredited or qualified
- Typically paired with Reg D 506(b)
- Look-through rules: if 10%+ of a 3(c)(1) fund is owned by another investment company, the underlying beneficial owners count toward the 100-person limit

### Section 3(c)(7) (15 U.S.C. § 80a-3(c)(7))
- **No investor limit** (but Reg D may impose practical limits)
- ALL investors must be **qualified purchasers** (not just accredited)
- No public offering
- Grandfather provision: existing 3(c)(1) fund can convert to 3(c)(7); up to 100 non-QP investors grandfathered

### Section 3(c)(5)(C) — Real Estate Funds
- Primarily engaged in purchasing/acquiring mortgages and other liens on real estate
- At least **55% of assets** in qualifying real estate interests
- At least **80% of assets** in qualifying real estate interests + real estate-related assets
- No investor number limit
- Used by REITs and real estate debt funds

## 5. Form D Filing Requirements

**Source**: SEC.gov — "Filing a Form D Notice" + 17 CFR 239.500

- File electronically via **EDGAR** within **15 calendar days** after first sale of securities
- Required for Rule 504, Rule 506(b), and Rule 506(c) offerings
- **Amendment required**:
  - Annually if offering continues beyond 12 months
  - Upon material change (as soon as practicable)
  - To correct material mistakes
- **Form D fields**: issuer info, related persons, offering details (type of securities, aggregate offering amount, amount sold, number of investors, exemption(s) claimed), sales compensation info, use of proceeds
- No filing fee to SEC (but state blue sky filings may have fees)
- Failure to file does NOT void the exemption (but may trigger SEC enforcement)

### State Blue Sky Notice Filings
- Most states require notice filing within 15 days of first sale to state residents
- **Reg D 506 offerings preempt state registration** (National Securities Markets Improvement Act) but states can require:
  - Notice filing (typically copy of Form D)
  - Filing fee ($100–$750 depending on state)
  - Consent to service of process
- States that commonly require notice filings: CA, NY, TX, FL, IL, MA, CT, NJ, PA
- Some states (e.g., Montana) don't require notice filings for 506 offerings

## 6. Anti-Fraud Requirements

### Rule 10b-5 (17 CFR 240.10b-5)
Applies to ALL securities offerings, exempt or not:
- Unlawful to employ any device, scheme, or artifice to defraud
- Unlawful to make untrue statement of material fact or omit material fact necessary to make statements not misleading
- Unlawful to engage in any act, practice, or course of business which operates as a fraud

### Section 17(a) Securities Act (15 U.S.C. § 77q(a))
- Similar to 10b-5 but applies specifically to offers and sales
- Subsection (1): scienter required (intent to defraud)
- Subsections (2) and (3): negligence standard sufficient

### Practical PPM Requirements to Avoid Liability
Even though Reg D doesn't mandate specific disclosures to accredited investors, anti-fraud rules require that any information provided be:
- **Accurate and not misleading**
- **Complete** regarding material facts
- PPMs typically include: risk factors, use of proceeds, management bios, conflicts of interest, financial information, tax consequences, prior performance (with proper disclaimers)

## 7. Document Types — Required Content

### Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)
Standard sections:
1. **Cover page** — fund name, offering amount, minimum investment, exemption type, legend ("These securities have not been registered...")
2. **Summary of terms** — fund type, GP, investment strategy, target return, fee structure
3. **Risk factors** — market risk, illiquidity, loss of capital, concentration, leverage, regulatory, tax, key person, conflicts of interest
4. **Use of proceeds** — how capital will be deployed, management fee allocation, organizational expenses
5. **Management** — bios of GP principals, track record, other funds managed, board composition
6. **Investment strategy** — thesis, sector focus, geographic focus, target deal size
7. **Terms of the offering** — classes of interests, capital commitments, drawdown procedures, distribution waterfall, clawback
8. **Conflicts of interest** — allocation of opportunities, co-investment, GP other activities
9. **Tax considerations** — partnership taxation, UBTI (for tax-exempt investors), FIRPTA (for foreign investors), state taxes, carried interest taxation
10. **ERISA considerations** — plan asset rules, VCOC/REOC exemptions
11. **Subscription procedures** — how to invest, minimum commitment, closing mechanics
12. **Legal matters** — governing law, dispute resolution, amendments, confidentiality

### Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA) / LLC Operating Agreement
Key provisions (Delaware RULPA 6 Del.C. Chapter 17):
- Formation and purpose
- Capital contributions and commitments
- Capital accounts and allocations (tax and economic)
- Distribution waterfall (preferred return → catch-up → carried interest split)
- Management fee (rate, basis, timing)
- Clawback provision (GP returns excess carry)
- Key person provision (suspension of investment period if key persons depart)
- Investment restrictions and limitations
- Valuation methodology
- Advisory committee composition and authority
- Transfer restrictions (ROFR, consent requirements)
- No-fault removal/dissolution provisions
- Term and extensions
- Indemnification and exculpation
- Confidentiality
- Side letter disclosure (MFN provisions)

### Subscription Agreement
- Investor representations: accredited/qualified status, investment experience, access to information, independent decision
- Suitability: ability to bear loss, liquidity needs, investment horizon
- ERISA status
- Tax status (US/non-US, tax-exempt)
- AML/KYC information
- Commitment amount
- Signature and acceptance

### Investor Questionnaire (Accreditation Verification)
**For 506(b)**: Self-certification with checkboxes for applicable criteria
**For 506(c)**: Must include verification mechanism:
- Income: request to provide W-2/1040/K-1 for prior 2 years
- Net worth: request for bank/brokerage statements + credit report
- Professional: request for license/certification number
- Third-party letter: space for attorney/CPA/adviser written confirmation

### Side Letter
Common provisions:
- MFN (most favored nation) — right to elect terms given to other LPs
- Fee discount or waiver
- Co-investment rights
- Enhanced reporting / transparency
- Excuse rights (opt out of specific investments)
- Key person modifications
- Transfer rights
- Regulatory compliance carve-outs (ERISA, Volcker Rule)

## 8. Fund Economics Norms (Market Standard 2024-2026)

| Term | Typical Range | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| Management fee | **1.5–2.0%** | On committed capital during investment period; on invested capital (or lower rate) after |
| Carried interest | **20%** standard | 25-30% for top-quartile or specialist managers |
| Preferred return (hurdle) | **8%** | Compound annual; whole-fund or deal-by-deal |
| GP commitment | **1–5%** of fund size | Shows alignment; 2-3% most common |
| Fund term | **10 years** | +2 one-year extensions typical |
| Investment period | **5 years** | Commitment period for new deals |
| Clawback | **Yes** (standard) | GP returns excess carry at fund wind-down; often net of taxes paid |
| Key person | **Suspension** of investment period if named key persons depart or reduce time commitment below threshold (typically 50-75%) |
| No-fault removal | Requires **75%+ LP vote** (by commitment) to remove GP without cause |
| Advisory committee | **3-7 members** from largest LPs; reviews conflicts, valuations, certain consents |
| Organizational expenses | **Capped at $500K–$1M** for mid-market; paid by fund |
| Placement agent fees | **1-2%** of commitments raised; paid by GP (not fund) |
| Waterfall | European (whole-fund) preferred for LPs; American (deal-by-deal) preferred by GPs |
